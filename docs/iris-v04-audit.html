Team Size — Problems at Each Scale Under / Ideal / Over

2 People (Current) — Understaffed

  • - Every person fills 5-7 roles
  • - Context-switching kills deep work
  • - One illness = 50% team loss
  • - No QA = bugs ship undetected
  • - No marketing = zero audience growth
  • - Burnout inevitable at current pace
  • - 12-18 month timeline to ship
  • - Demo crashes go uncaught

4-5 People — Right-Sized

  • + Programmer focuses on code only
  • + Dedicated artist, no biz distractions
  • + Part-time QA catches demo crashes
  • + Part-time producer tracks scope
  • + Sound design gets proper attention
  • + Marketing starts building wishlists
  • + 6-9 month timeline to ship
  • + Bus factor improves to 3+

8+ People — Overstaffed

  • - Burn rate hits $30-50K/month
  • - Communication overhead explodes
  • - Funding runway drops to 1-2 months
  • - Management overhead needs a producer
  • - Decision paralysis on creative vision
  • - Revenue split reduces per-person ROI
  • - Scope creep accelerates ("we have people")
  • - Indie agility destroyed

Build the Disappearance.

The horror twist is Iris's entire market identity. Every funding application, publisher pitch, trailer beat, and press headline depends on the moment a player realizes what Nema has been doing. The simulation half is solid. The horror half — dates vanishing, souvenirs accumulating, newspaper ads going silent — is what turns "another dating sim" into "the game where you slowly realize you're a serial killer." Ship that, and the rest is execution.
